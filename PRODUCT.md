# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Three overlapping customer types: (1) The skincare convert — 25–35, was using Clicks moisturiser, got into ingredient research via Reddit or TikTok, now wants real actives and trusts labels over marketing copy. (2) The gift buyer — buying for a partner or sibling, needs curation and brand confidence, won't read an ingredient list but will pay a premium for trust. (3) The existing K-beauty fan — already imports from YesStyle or Jolse, choosing GLOW for local stock, no customs delays, and grey-market protection. Context: most browsing happens on mobile, mid-routine or during a lunch break. Purchases are considered, not impulse.

## Product Purpose

GLOW sells authentic Korean skincare in South Africa, organised around the 7-step routine that makes it work. The store exists because grey-market K-beauty is a genuine problem locally: old stock, heat-damaged formulas, outright fakes. GLOW buys direct from Seoul-licensed distributors, batch-verifies every unit, and ships from Johannesburg. Success means a customer trusts GLOW enough to build their whole routine here, then reorders.

## Brand Personality

Confident, direct, slightly irreverent. Expert knowledge without condescension. Not Seoul-exported, not London-polished: grounded in Joburg, warm climate, practical. The friend who actually read the studies and will tell you what works, and what is overhyped.

## Anti-references

- **Clinical whiteness**: CeraVe, Paula's Choice territory. All-white surfaces, pharmaceutical sans-serif, ingredient charts as visual decoration.
- **SaaS landing page**: gradient hero, floating orbs, metric trust-badges, "Start your skincare journey" CTA energy.
- **Luxury fashion house**: Chanel/La Mer. All-caps Didot, impenetrable whitespace, feels expensive rather than knowledgeable.
- **Generic K-beauty cute**: pink gradients, soft pastel section backgrounds, cherry blossom motifs, "glow up" in italic script. No local character.

## Design Principles

1. **Expertise earns the space.** Information density is a feature, not a problem. The person who reads the ingredient list is the primary customer. Design for her first.
2. **South African, not Seoul-exported.** The aesthetic should feel grounded in Joburg: warm, practical, direct. Not a k-drama prop, not a London boutique.
3. **The product is the proof.** The formulas, batch numbers, and supplier names are the brand statement. No dewey-skin lifestyle clichés.
4. **Confident understatement.** Doesn't shout. Knows it doesn't need to. Restraint is intentional, not timid.
5. **Ritual, not routine.** The 7-step structure is an educational framework. Each step earns its place in the design and in the customer's bathroom.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. All interactive elements keyboard accessible. Reduced-motion preference respected. Color contrast maintained across all background variants. ZAR pricing prominent throughout.
