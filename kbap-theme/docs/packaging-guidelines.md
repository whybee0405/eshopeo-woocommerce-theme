# K-BAP Packaging Guidelines

## Packaging Direction

Packaging should feel natural, practical, and trusted. It should look ready for local markets and future supermarket shelves without becoming loud or overly commercial.

## Core Rules

- Keep the gimbap-slice logo concept intact.
- Use the official palette.
- Use large clear product names.
- Use plain product descriptions.
- Prioritise legibility.
- Avoid fake craft textures.
- Avoid neon, anime, novelty graphics, and overdecorated Korean motifs.

## Materials

Preferred:

- Matte labels.
- Warm off-white paper.
- Clear jars where the product should be visible.
- Kraft only when it feels functional, not rustic by default.
- Recyclable or reusable formats where feasible.

## Kimchi

Front label:

- K-BAP logo.
- Product name.
- Pack size.
- Short flavour note.
- Storage instruction.

Back label:

- Ingredients.
- Allergens.
- Storage.
- Best before.
- Batch number.
- Manufacturer details.
- Serving ideas.

Visual style:

- Rice background.
- Seaweed text.
- Kimchi accent.
- Small Perilla or Sesame detail only if useful.

## Meal Kits

Front:

- Dish name.
- "Korean meal kit".
- Serves count.
- Cooking time.
- Key inclusions.

Back:

- Step-by-step preparation.
- What is included.
- What to add at home.
- Storage.
- Allergens.

## Sauces

Use a simple label system with colour-coded accents:

- Kimchi for spicy sauces.
- Sesame for savoury sauces.
- Perilla for fresh or herb-led sauces.

## Frozen Products

Requirements:

- Strong product name.
- Clear frozen storage instruction.
- Cooking method.
- Safety note.
- Best before and batch area.

## Takeaway Packaging

Use minimal branded stickers rather than fully printed packaging at early scale.

Formats:

- Bowl labels.
- Tray seals.
- Sauce pot labels.
- Tamper-evident stickers.
- Thank-you card.

## Boxes

Use for catering packages, meal kits, and gift sets.

Rules:

- Logo on top.
- Product label on side.
- No busy repeat pattern.
- Include handling notes when needed.

## Bags

Use Rice or unbleached natural material with Seaweed logo. Keep one side clean.

## Stickers

Create small labels for:

- Kimchi.
- Spicy.
- Vegetarian.
- Keep chilled.
- Heating instructions.
- Made by K-BAP.

## Business Cards

Front:

- Logo.
- Name.
- Role if needed.

Back:

- Website.
- Email.
- Phone.
- Social handle.

## Gift Cards

Use food photography on one side and a quiet brand panel on the other.

## Aprons And Uniforms

- Seaweed or warm neutral apron.
- Small logo placement.
- No large slogans.
- Durable embroidery preferred.

## Print Checks

Before production:

- Test logo at small size.
- Check contrast under supermarket lighting.
- Confirm barcode quiet zone.
- Confirm legal food labelling requirements.
- Print one physical sample before a full run.
