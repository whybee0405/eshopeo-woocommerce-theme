# K-BAP SEO Strategy

## SEO Goals

- Rank for Korean catering searches in South Africa.
- Make the HTML menu discoverable.
- Prepare K-BAP Kimchi, meal kits, sauces, and future products for ecommerce search.
- Build trust around authentic Korean food.
- Support local discovery for Johannesburg and broader South Africa queries.

## Primary Topics

- Korean catering South Africa.
- Korean catering Johannesburg.
- Korean fried chicken catering.
- Gimbap catering.
- Tteokbokki catering.
- Korean food catering.
- Korean BBQ catering.
- Kimchi South Africa.
- Korean meal kits South Africa.

## Local SEO

Actions:

- Add complete business details when available.
- Use consistent NAP data across website, Google Business Profile, social platforms, and directories.
- Create location-aware copy without stuffing keywords.
- Add service area language.
- Add event and catering content for Johannesburg and South Africa.

## Structured Data

Current theme already includes useful JSON-LD foundations. Expand and harden:

- Organization.
- WebSite with SearchAction.
- Menu.
- Product.
- FAQPage.
- BreadcrumbList.
- LocalBusiness or FoodEstablishment when address/service details are confirmed.
- Event only when specific public events are listed.

## Menu SEO

The menu page should remain HTML, not PDF-only.

Each section should include:

- Descriptive category heading.
- Dish names.
- Short descriptions.
- Dietary or heat notes where useful.
- Internal links to catering and related products.

## Product SEO

Every product should include:

- Product name.
- Product type.
- Short description.
- Full description.
- Serving or pack size.
- Storage notes.
- Ingredients where available.
- Allergen information.
- Product image alt text.
- Product schema.

## Kimchi SEO

Create a dedicated kimchi page before retail expansion.

Target themes:

- K-BAP Kimchi.
- Korean kimchi South Africa.
- How to serve kimchi.
- Kimchi with rice, fried chicken, stews, gimbap, and fried rice.
- Pack sizes and availability.

## Meal Kit SEO

Create category and product pages for:

- Bulgogi meal kit.
- Korean short rib stew kit.
- Japchae kit.
- Korean cooking kit South Africa.

Content should explain what is included, what customers need at home, cooking time, storage, and serving ideas.

## Catering SEO

Create content around:

- Corporate lunches.
- Cultural events.
- Embassy and institutional events.
- Festivals and markets.
- Private events.
- Mixed guest tables.

Avoid naming private clients or events that cannot be named.

## Internal Linking

Core links:

- Homepage to menu.
- Homepage to catering.
- Homepage to kimchi.
- Menu to catering.
- Catering to menu.
- Product pages to related dishes.
- Blog posts to product and catering pages.
- FAQ to catering and contact.

## Content Strategy

Recommended articles:

- What to order for a Korean catering table.
- Korean fried chicken for events.
- Gimbap, samgak gimbap, and when to serve each.
- How to serve kimchi at home.
- Tteokbokki heat guide.
- Bulgogi meal kit serving ideas.
- Korean food for South African office lunches.

## Metadata Rules

- Titles should be specific and under control.
- Descriptions should mention dish, service, and South Africa where relevant.
- Avoid generic "best food experience" language.
- Every image needs useful alt text.

## Measurement

Track:

- Organic traffic to menu.
- Catering enquiry conversions.
- Product page impressions.
- Search terms for kimchi and meal kits.
- Local discovery queries.
- Core Web Vitals.
