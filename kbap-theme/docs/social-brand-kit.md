# K-BAP Social Brand Kit

## Social Direction

Social should feel like real food from real events. It should not feel like generic restaurant marketing or trend content.

## Platforms

### Instagram

Primary visual channel for food, catering tables, kimchi, markets, and launches.

### Facebook

Useful for local events, markets, announcements, and community updates.

### LinkedIn

Use for corporate catering, institutional trust, cultural events, and product growth.

### TikTok

Use for short food preparation, packing, event setup, and market day clips. Keep it simple and real.

## Photography Rules

Do:

- Shoot in natural light.
- Show real food texture.
- Use wood, stone, ceramic, and simple table settings.
- Show hands only when they add warmth.
- Photograph catering tables before guests arrive.
- Show kimchi jars clearly.

Do not:

- Use fake steam.
- Over-saturate reds.
- Use HDR.
- Use novelty props.
- Add neon effects.
- Use generic K-pop or anime styling.

## Post Templates

### Dish Feature

- Large food image.
- Dish name.
- One short description.
- Optional serving note.

### Catering Recap

- Event type if public or approved.
- Short table description.
- Key dishes.
- Soft CTA for future catering.

### Kimchi Post

- Jar or serving image.
- Taste note.
- Serving idea.
- Availability note.

### Market Announcement

- Date.
- Location.
- Menu highlights.
- Stock note.

### Educational Post

- One dish or ingredient.
- Simple explanation.
- Serving suggestion.

## Story Templates

- Today's menu.
- Event prep.
- Sold out note.
- Market location.
- Quick poll for future products.
- Behind the table.

## Reel Rules

- 6 to 20 seconds.
- Natural sound or quiet music.
- Show one action clearly.
- Avoid fast cuts that make food hard to inspect.
- Add captions for key information.

## Highlight Covers

Use simple covers:

- Menu.
- Catering.
- Kimchi.
- Markets.
- Events.
- FAQ.

Use Seaweed line icons or simple text on Rice background. Do not over-illustrate.

## LinkedIn Tone

Use calm credibility:

- "K-BAP catered a cultural event in Johannesburg."
- "The menu was planned for Korean and non-Korean guests."
- "For corporate or institutional catering, enquiries are open."

Avoid hype and vague thought leadership.

## Visual Consistency

- Keep Rice backgrounds and Seaweed text for designed posts.
- Use Kimchi accent sparingly.
- Do not place text over busy food areas.
- Keep product names readable on mobile.
